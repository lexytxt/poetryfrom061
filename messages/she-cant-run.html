She can’t run

She can’t be on those
street corners anymore.

She can say,
“What’s done is done.”

She can pull the trigger
of that empty gun.

She can hide from the past,
but she can’t run.

When the only person left
to root for her is her.

She just can’t be on those
street corners anymore.
